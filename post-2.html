Google Veo 3.1 Targets YouTube Shorts with Native Vertical Video

January 20, 2026

Google has officially announced Veo 3.1, a major update designed for the mobile-first creator. While much of the hype is around entertainment, the ability to generate native vertical video (9:16 aspect ratio) has significant implications for corporate social media and marketing workflows.

The Breakthrough: "Ingredients to Video"

The most notable feature for corporate users is the "Ingredients to Video" workflow. This allows creators to upload a "reference character"—such as a company mascot or a specific employee—and a "reference style." Veo 3.1 then maintains that identity across different settings, finally solving the "character consistency" problem that has previously limited AI video usage in professional branding.

The "Accounting" Take

From a documentation and internal communications standpoint, this tool allows large organizations to scale their "influencer-style" internal marketing. For example, a finance department could use a consistent digital "guide" to walk employees through new compliance procedures or expense reporting software in a short, vertical format that is easy to consume on mobile devices.

"The ability to maintain brand identity across an infinite number of situations is a game changer for scaling professional-grade content."

By integrating this into the YouTube Create app, Google is making professional-grade AI video accessible to casual creators and corporate staff alike, bypassing the need for expensive external production for simple internal announcements.
