Google Veo 3.1 Targets YouTube Shorts with Native Vertical Video

January 20, 2026

Just this week, Google announced Veo 3.1, specifically optimized for the mobile-first creator. The update introduces native support for the 9:16 aspect ratio, allowing creators to generate high-quality vertical content for YouTube Shorts and TikTok without the quality loss associated with cropping landscape footage.

The Details

A standout feature is the improved "Ingredients to Video" workflow. Users can now upload a "reference character" (a still photo of a person or mascot) and a "reference style," and Veo 3.1 will generate a video that maintains that specific identity across different settings. This effectively solves the "character consistency" problem that has plagued AI creators for years.

My Take

By integrating Veo 3.1 into its YouTube Create app, Google makes professional-grade AI video available to millions of casual creators. The “Ingredients to Video” feature is a game changer for brands who want to scale influencer marketing; they can now easily insert their favorite brand mascot into an infinite number of situations.
